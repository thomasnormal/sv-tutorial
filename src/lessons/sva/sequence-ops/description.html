SVA provides four operators for composing two sequences:

  • s1 and s2 — both must match; they start together but may end at different cycles
  • s1 or s2 — at least one must match
  • s1 intersect s2 — both must match and end at exactly the same cycle
  • s1 within s2 — s1 starts and ends entirely inside s2's window

intersect is the most useful for bus protocols: it checks that two independent conditions hold simultaneously for the same duration:

valid |-> (valid[*4]) intersect (ready[*4])

This says: if valid is high, then both valid and ready must stay high for exactly 4 consecutive cycles — they must be in lock-step.

Use and when two conditions must both eventually be satisfied but may take different amounts of time. Use intersect when they must finish together — common for data/valid or valid/ready handshake verification.
