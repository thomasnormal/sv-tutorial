Individual coverpoints track each signal independently. Cross coverage tracks combinations — it creates a bin for every pair of values from two coverpoints and checks that each combination was seen:

The cross syntax is simply: name: cross cp1, cp2; — it automatically creates one bin for every combination of bins from the two coverpoints.

For the SRAM, crossing cp_addr × cp_we produces 32 bins (16 addresses × 2 operations). Any unhit bin is an address that was never read or never written during the test run.

Open mem_coverage.sv. The two coverpoints are already written. Add a cross of cp_addr and cp_we inside the covergroup.

Cross coverage can grow explosively with more coverpoints or wider bins. It is common to exclude impossible or uninteresting combinations with ignore_bins or illegal_bins to focus coverage effort where it matters.
